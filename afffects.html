Affects on Privacy without Net Neutrality

Here are just a few of the affects on privacy:

  1. ISPs can monitor what websites you go to.
  2. ISPs will be able to see what you upload. (ie. photos or videos)
  3. ISPs will be able to see when you post something or send something. (ie. any social media post or email)
  4. ISPs will also be able to see the exact url of everything you visit. (ie. They will know the exact pages you go to.)

Affects on Usage without Net Neutrality

Here are some of the affects on usage:

  1. ISPs can charge you depnding on what your doing. (ie. If your watching Netflix they can charge you more than if you are reading from 9news.)
  2. ISPs can block websites that don't reflect their veiws. (ie. Lets say an internet company is far left, they could block all the websites that lean right.)
  3. ISPs will be able to charge you based on the amount of data you use per month. (ie. Essentially it would be like phone plans right now.)
  4. You also may experience slow downs on certain websites because a company can't afford to pay the ISP to be in their fast lane. This fast would make all the companies that pay for load their websites faster, while slowing down everyone else.

  2. Our right to speak freely will also be violated! We won't be able to communicate without paying for special packages. Also small blogs won't be able to start up because theyy won't be able to afford to be in the "Fast Lane".
